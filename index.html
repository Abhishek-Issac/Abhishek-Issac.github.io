/01 · about

A self-taught tech nerd.

Anyone can spot I’m a tech nerd easily. I’m the kid who turns a friend’s bricked OnePlus 10T back on at 1 AM, then writes a Pyrogram bot that mass-rebroadcasts movies to a million people before sunrise.

I’m Abhishek Issac — sixteen, based in Kerala, India, somewhere between Wayanad’s two cell towers and a Kollam couriered package. I’ve been writing code since I was twelve, mostly in Python, mostly via Termux on a phone, long before I owned a real laptop.

My speciality is Telegram automation at scale — Pyrogram, MongoDB caching with TTLs, PostgreSQL for the long-term, asynchronous everything. I’ve shipped bots for movies, voice-chat security, force-subscribe gates, file batching, scorecard generators and small games — many of them still running on Easypanel, Heroku, Koyeb, or my own DigitalOcean box.

When I’m not pushing commits I’m flashing a custom ROM (currently crDroid Android 16 with KernelSU root), benchmarking phones I shouldn’t be buying, beating Wuthering Waves on an SD860, or cramming for my +1 BioScience exams. I’m really bad at knowing people — “a black sheep in a white farm” — but I’m really good at knowing systems.

I read code the way other people read novels, and I’d rather minify a two-thousand-line plugin than write a five-line cover letter. This site is that minify.

Cavin VC Guard

Pyrogram · MTProto rawBot + UserbotMongo TTL cache

Automated voice-chat moderator for Telegram. A dual-client architecture (Bot + Userbot) monitors group calls in real time and enforces join rules, streaming permissions and anti-abuse policies. A short, non-linear timeline.

Most of these dates are approximate — I learned by jumping between things, not by following a syllabus.

EARLY · ~2019

First lines of code, on a phone.

Started writing tiny Python scripts inside Termux, before I had a real computer. Half of it was copy-paste; the other half was confusion-driven learning.

NOV · 2021

Joined GitHub as @Abhishek-Issac.

Account created on 2021-11-08. Pushed shell scripts, half-broken file-share bots, and a frankly absurd number of forks I used to read other people’s code.

2022

First real Telegram bots — an EvaMaria fork, an autobatch generator, and a series-fetcher that taught me regex the hard way.

2023

Channels and bots crossed enough users that I had to actually think about caching, TTLs, indexes and rate-limiting. Telegram’s flood errors became my mortal enemy.

2024

Postgres + production hygiene.

Migrated Cavin Game from MongoDB to PostgreSQL with a sync bridge. Wrote my first proper backup/restore commands, JSONB settings, score-log tables.

2025

Custom ROMs as a lifestyle.

crDroid A16 + KernelSU on the Poco X3 Pro becomes my daily setup. Magisk modules for school apps. EDL flashes for friends’ bricked OnePluses at midnight.

2026 · NOW

+1 BioScience  ·  this site.

Cramming biology in one window, refactoring bots in another, redesigning my portfolio in a third. Still 16. Still going.

/07 · how I work

Build small. Ship fast. Refactor in public.

I don’t believe in waiting for the perfect architecture. I’d rather have a working version one in production tonight than a beautiful diagram on Monday. The commit graph is my notebook.

I optimize for two things: uptime for the users I take care of, and readability for the future me who has to debug this at 2 AM on a phone keyboard. Everything else is negotiable.

Ask me about codes. I’m awful at small talk and I have a million users in Telegram bots, groups and channels — that should give you a sense of where my attention has been for the last few years.
